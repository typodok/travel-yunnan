Stones and Flowers

Geological and Gastronomical Journey

Full Experience

Morning: After breakfast, depart for the Stone Forest — a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of Yunnan’s most iconic natural landmarks. Towering limestone formations rise like a forest of stone, shaped over millions of years by wind and water. Wander through ancient legends and surreal landscapes guided by stories of the Yi people.

Lunch: Enjoy a countryside lunch at a local farmhouse or Yi minority family home near the Stone Forest. Suggested dishes: roasted suckling pig, millet flatbread, free-range chicken soup, steamed dendrobium orchids, and locally brewed millet or corn liquor. Simple, authentic, and deeply rooted in the land.

Afternoon: On the way back to the city, stop at Dounan Flower Market — Asia’s largest fresh-cut flower trading center. Wander between flower stalls, artisanal florists, and lively markets, taking in the vibrant colors and delicate scents of Yunnan’s floral heritage. Optional hands-on flower arrangement experiences are also available.

Evening: Return to Kunming in the evening. Dinner is open to your own taste — whether you prefer classic Yunnan cuisine or modern fusion restaurants, the city offers no shortage of flavorful choices. We’re happy to make recommendations based on your preferences.

Group size: Max 10 people

Includes: Local guide, lunch, Stone Forest entry, transportation
